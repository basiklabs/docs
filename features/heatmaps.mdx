Heatmaps give you a visual summary of user behaviour across all sessions on a page. Instead of watching individual replays, you see the aggregate: the hottest spots, the ignored areas, and the scroll cutoff point where most users stop reading.

## What's tracked

### Click heatmaps

Every click is recorded (element, coordinates, timestamp, session). The heatmap view layers those clicks on top of a screenshot of your page, colour-coded from cool (few clicks) to hot (many clicks).

Use this to answer:
- Which CTA is actually getting clicked?
- Are users clicking things that aren't links?
- Is my primary button being ignored?

### Rage clicks

A rage click is when a user rapidly clicks the same spot multiple times — usually a sign of frustration. These are flagged separately in the dashboard so you can filter for them and watch the session to understand what's broken or confusing.

### Scroll depth

Tracelit tracks how far down the page each visitor scrolled. The aggregate is shown as a gradient bar on the right side of the heatmap — from 100% at the top (everyone sees this) down to 0% at the bottom (almost no one reaches this).

Use this to:
- Decide whether to move important content higher up
- Check whether your above-the-fold CTA is actually in the fold
- Understand if users are reading the full page before converting

## Viewing heatmaps

<img
  src="/images/heatmap-page-selector.png"
  alt="Tracelit heatmap page selector showing a list of pages with session counts"
  className="rounded-lg"
/>

In the dashboard, go to **Heatmaps** and select any page. Tracelit builds the heatmap from all sessions on that URL. You can:

- Filter by date range
- Filter by device type (desktop / mobile / tablet)
- Switch between click, move, and scroll views
- Click any hotspot to see the individual click events and jump to their sessions

## Form interaction tracking

Tracelit also tracks which form fields users interact with and where they abandon the form — which fields get filled, which get skipped, and which cause drop-off.

<Warning>
  Tracelit **never** captures field values. Only interaction events (focus, blur, change) are recorded. Passwords are always blocked at the DOM level.
</Warning>

## Zero config

Click tracking, rage click detection, scroll depth, and form interaction tracking are all on by default. No setup required beyond installing the tracker.
